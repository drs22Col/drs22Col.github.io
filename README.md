Setton is an M.A. candidate in the Quantitative Methods in the Social Sciences program at Columbia University. She worked for fifteen years as a staff editor and writer for national magazines like Natural History and Forbes. Her writing, which includes an eight-page article on neutrino physics, has appeared in Discover, Forbes and others, and has been featured on National Public Radio. Previously she was an analyst for Morningstar. She is researching a dual scientific biography which she is illustrating with digital art, watercolor paintings and collage. Her cartoons and illustrations have appeared in Natural History and Commonweal. She also teaches graduate-level writing at the New School University and holds an M.A. in English literature.
